Wait, Who Are You?

Apologies for my lack of manners! Allow me to introduce myself. My name is Marina White, and I am a video game developer who aspires to use my skills in video game programming, game design, game user research, and technical artistry to help create games that impact the heart and soul of all players.

How it all Began

I was first introduced to the wonderful world of video games when I was only four years old, with my first game console being the Nintendo DS lite. Though my catalogue of games grew gradually over the years, my love for video games grew exponentially with every new experience of play. Throughout my life, I discovered and fell in love with many different IPs, my favorites being Super Mario Bros, Bust-A-Move, Five Nights at Freddy's, Henry Stickman, Keep Talking and Nobody Explodes, and especially Splatoon and Sonic the Hedgehog. Three years ago, I never considered a possible future where I would become a video game developer myself, considering I had no computer science skills, nor did I know the first thing on how to design a game. Thankfully, with the opportunity presented to me by the University of Southern California, I am able to pursue my degree in Computer Science Games and minor in Technical Game Art, where in pursuit of my education I continue to develop my skills, talent, and creativity to bring my ideas to life.

How it's Going

Bernobyl

For faster reactors, I present to you this game with a rather unstable reactor! Before you is a panel of three modules that need constant attention in order to keep the reactor stable. Solve the modules' puzzles to deactivate it and maintain stability—ignore the module or make a mistake, and you will face a reactor meltdown. How long do you think you can survive?

Bernobyl was developed over the period of a fifteen-week semester, produced under the standard ideation -> preproduction -> alpha -> beta -> postproduction -> release production timeline. This game was developed by me, alongside my partner developer, Martin Gong. I was the lead designer and artist for this project, and was responsible for handling the communication and logistics with my audio team from the Berklee College of Music.

These other works here are smaller solo projects, all meant to be recreations of exhisting games. Each game placed emphasis on a different fundamental aspect of video game programming at the time of its development.
